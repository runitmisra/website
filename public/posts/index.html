Building a Go CI pipeline in an offline environment

2023-05-25 :: Runit Misra
In today’s interconnected world, the majority of software development and deployment processes heavily rely on internet connectivity. However, certain organizations dealing with highly sensitive data cannot afford to expose their infrastructure to the internet or must tightly control internet access. This is where Offline environments, also known as Airgapped environments, come into play. Offline environments refer to infrastructure with limited or no connectivity to the open internet. While they offer enhanced security and independence from internet availability, they pose unique challenges for building CI (Continuous Integration) pipelines, especially when it comes to managing third-party dependencies.
